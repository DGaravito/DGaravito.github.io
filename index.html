Hello, I am David Garavito, a Interprofessional Polytrauma & Traumatic Brain Injury Rehabilitation Research Fellow for the Department of Veterans Affairs! I received my M.A. in Human Development from Cornell University in 2017, my J.D. from Cornell Law School in 2020, and my Ph.D. in Human Development from Cornell University in 2021. My graduate work was under Dr. Valerie Reyna in the Laboratory for Rational Decision Making. My research interests primarily lie in cognitive theory and neuroscience, with my main focus on judgment and decision making. My research examines the perception of risks and risk taking within various contexts (sports, substance use, vaccination, etc.) and the cognitive and neural effects of concussive and sub-concussive injuries. I also conduct research on memory across the lifespan and am using advanced models and theory to detect the development and progression of possible neurodegenerative diseases in at-risk populations. Finally, as a graduate of Cornell Law School and a barred attorney in New York, my legal experience has provided me with the insight to write on legal and policy matters relating to criminal law, health disparities, race and socioeconomic status, and psychology and neuroscience.
